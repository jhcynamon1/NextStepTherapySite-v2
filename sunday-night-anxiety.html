When Sunday Becomes Your Least Favorite Day

It's not just "hating your job." It's a physiological stress response that ruins your recovery time.

The 3 PM Spiral

That familiar knot in your stomach starts forming in the afternoon. Your brain begins cataloging tomorrow's to-do list before the weekend is even over.

The Insomnia

You're exhausted, but you can't sleep. You lie in bed replaying conversations or worrying about emails you haven't even received yet.

The "Monday Dread"

You can't be present with your family or friends because a part of your brain is already at the office, bracing for impact.

How We Fix It (Without Quitting Your Job)

Boundary Setting

Learn specific psychological tools to "close the door" on work Friday at 5 PM and keep it closed until Monday morning.

Process, Don't Suppress

We use ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy) to help you handle work stress in real-time so it doesn't stockpile for the weekend.

The "Ramp Up" Routine

Create a Sunday evening routine that signals safety to your nervous system, rather than panic.

Need Relief Right Now?

Try this: The "Brain Dump" Protocol. Before bed, write down every work worry for exactly 5 minutes. Then close the notebook and put it in another room. It signals to your brain: "It's handled."
