What to Expect from Therapy

If you've never been to therapy before, you might be wondering what actually happens in sessions. Here's what the process typically looks like when we work together:

Your Free 15-Minute Consultation

We'll start with a brief phone call where you can ask questions and get a sense of whether we might work well together. This isn't therapyit's a conversation about what you're experiencing and how I might be able to help. No pressure, no commitment beyond that initial conversation.

First Session: Understanding Your Experience

Your first full session (usually 50 minutes) focuses on understanding your specific situation. What's bringing you to therapy? What's been working in your life and what feels challenging? How have these issues been affecting your daily life, relationships, and overall well-being?

Building Your Toolkit: Early Sessions

This is where we develop practical strategies you can use immediately while also understanding the deeper patterns that keep problems stuck. I use evidence-based approaches like Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), which helps you develop psychological flexibilitythe ability to stay present with difficult experiences while still making choices based on your values.

Mindfulness Skills

Practical ways to stay grounded and present during stressful moments in your Ontario daily life.

Cognitive Flexibility

Learning to notice thoughts and feelings without automatically being controlled by them.

Behavioral Strategies

Gradually testing out new behaviors to build confidence and tolerance for discomfort.

Values Clarification

Identifying what truly matters to you so you can make choices based on values rather than fears.

Ongoing Growth and Integration

As you practice these new approaches, we'll refine them based on what works best for your lifestyle and personality. Some clients prefer weekly sessions for several months, while others prefer biweekly sessions over a longer period. We'll find a rhythm that supports your growth.

The goal isn't to need therapy foreverit's to develop skills you can use independently. Many clients find that after developing these foundations, they can manage challenges much more effectively on their own, checking in occasionally for tune-ups during particularly stressful periods.

"Is virtual therapy as effective as in-person?"

Research consistently shows that virtual therapy is just as effective as in-person therapy for anxiety, depression, and most mental health concerns. Many clients actually prefer the comfort and convenience of virtual sessions.

"How do I know if my problems are 'serious enough' for therapy?"

If your challenges are affecting your daily life, relationships, work, or overall well-being, therapy can help. You don't need to wait for a crisisearly support often prevents bigger problems later.

That's completely normal and something I help with. I'll ask questions that help you explore your experiences, and we'll follow what feels most relevant and helpful for you in each session.

This varies by person and situation. Some people feel significantly better after a few sessions, while others prefer longer-term support. We'll regularly check in about your progress and adjust as needed.

"Will my insurance cover virtual therapy sessions?"

"Is my information kept confidential?"

Absolutely. As a Registered Psychotherapist, I'm bound by strict confidentiality requirements. What we discuss stays between us, with very limited exceptions related to safety as required by law.
