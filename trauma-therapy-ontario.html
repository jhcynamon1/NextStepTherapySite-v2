Understanding Trauma Through a Healing Lens

Trauma therapy recognizes that traumatic experiences can profoundly impact how we relate to ourselves, others, and the world. Rather than focusing solely on symptoms, our approach emphasizes your inherent capacity for healing and growth.

Trauma can manifest in various ways:

  • Intrusive memories or flashbacks
  • Hypervigilance and difficulty feeling safe
  • Emotional numbness or overwhelming emotions
  • Sleep disturbances and nightmares
  • Avoidance of trauma-related triggers
  • Difficulty trusting others or forming relationships

Trauma-Informed ACT Approach

Our trauma therapy utilizes Acceptance and Commitment Therapy adapted for trauma experiences. This evidence-based approach helps you develop psychological flexibility while honoring the protective function of trauma responses.

Key therapeutic elements include:

  • Building present-moment awareness and grounding skills
  • Developing acceptance of difficult trauma-related emotions
  • Creating psychological distance from traumatic memories
  • Reconnecting with personal values and meaning
  • Gradual engagement with avoided experiences
  • Strengthening sense of self beyond trauma identity

Types of Trauma We Address

Professional trauma counseling recognizes that trauma experiences vary widely in nature, severity, and impact. Our approach adapts to your specific healing needs while maintaining core therapeutic principles.

Areas of trauma specialization:

  • Single-incident trauma and PTSD
  • Complex trauma and developmental difficulties
  • Workplace trauma and professional incidents
  • Medical trauma and health-related stress
  • Relationship trauma and betrayal
  • Grief and loss-related trauma

Creating Safety in Virtual Trauma Therapy

Virtual trauma therapy can offer unique advantages for trauma healing, including increased sense of control, familiarity of environment, and reduced triggers associated with traveling to appointments. We prioritize creating emotional safety within the virtual space.

Post-Traumatic Growth and Resilience

While trauma can create lasting impacts, trauma therapy also recognizes the potential for post-traumatic growth. This includes developing deeper relationships, increased appreciation for life, spiritual growth, and discovering inner strength.

Collaborative and Client-Centered Approach

Effective trauma treatment recognizes you as the expert on your own experience. Therapy focuses on restoring your sense of choice and control while providing professional guidance and evidence-based tools for healing.
