Evidence-Based Approach

Understanding Acceptance & Commitment Therapy

ACT is a mindfulness-based therapy that helps you develop psychological flexibility - the ability to stay present with your experience while taking action guided by your values, even when facing difficult thoughts and emotions.

Psychological Flexibility

Learn to be present with difficult emotions without being controlled by them. Develop the ability to adapt your behavior based on what's most important to you in each situation.

Values-Based Living

Identify what truly matters to you and use these values as your guide for action. Move beyond just reducing symptoms to living a meaningful, fulfilling life.

Mindfulness & Acceptance

Develop present-moment awareness and learn to accept difficult thoughts and feelings rather than struggling against them. Notice your internal experience without judgment.

Committed Action

Take concrete steps toward your values even when you don't feel like it. Build patterns of behavior that create the life you want to live.

Core Principles

The Six ACT Processes

Present Moment Awareness

Practice mindfulness to stay connected to the here-and-now. Learn to observe your thoughts and feelings without getting caught up in them.

Acceptance

Stop struggling against difficult emotions and experiences. Allow them to exist without trying to control, avoid, or eliminate them.

Cognitive Defusion

Learn to see thoughts as just thoughts, not facts. Create distance from unhelpful thinking patterns and reduce their impact on your behavior.

Self-as-Context

Develop a flexible sense of self that isn't defined by your thoughts, emotions, or experiences. Access the part of you that can observe all your internal experiences.

Values Clarification

Identify what you truly care about across different life domains. Use your values as a compass for making decisions and taking action.

Committed Action

Set goals and take steps that align with your values, even in the presence of difficult thoughts and feelings. Build momentum toward meaningful change.

Effective Treatment

What ACT Can Help With

Anxiety & Worry

Learn to accept anxious thoughts and feelings while taking valued action. Break cycles of avoidance and live fully despite anxiety.

Depression & Low Mood

Reconnect with activities and relationships that matter to you. Move beyond just managing symptoms to creating a meaningful life.

Chronic Stress

Develop resilience and flexibility in how you respond to stressful situations. Learn to stay grounded in your values during difficult times.

Life Transitions

Navigate major changes with clarity about what matters most to you. Use transitions as opportunities to align your life with your values.

Relationship Issues

Improve relationships by staying present and acting according to your values around connection and intimacy, even during conflict.

Work & Career Stress

Find meaning and purpose in your professional life. Balance career ambitions with other important values like family and personal well-being.

Unique Approach

How ACT Is Different

Focus on Values, Not Just Symptoms

While other therapies focus primarily on reducing symptoms, ACT emphasizes living meaningfully according to your values, even in the presence of difficult emotions.

Acceptance Rather Than Control

Instead of trying to eliminate or control difficult thoughts and feelings, ACT teaches acceptance and psychological flexibility as paths to wellbeing.

Mindfulness-Based

Incorporates mindfulness practices to develop present-moment awareness and observe your thoughts and feelings without being controlled by them.
