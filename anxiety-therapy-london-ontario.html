Comprehensive Anxiety Treatment in London, Ontario

"I can't even drive down Wonderland Road anymore without having a panic attack." This was how Sarah, a London client, described her anxiety when we first met. Living in London brings unique stressors—from navigating the chaos around Western University during student season, to dealing with endless construction on the 401, to managing the economic uncertainty that affects so many Southwestern Ontario communities.

After working with hundreds of London area residents, I've learned that anxiety here isn't just about brain chemistry—it's about the daily realities of living in a mid-sized city trying to maintain its identity between Toronto's sprawl and Detroit's industrial legacy. Whether you're a Western student overwhelmed by academic pressure, a healthcare worker at Victoria Hospital dealing with burnout, or someone in Woodstock or St. Thomas feeling isolated from mental health resources, I understand the specific triggers that affect our community.

Our Evidence-Based Anxiety Treatment Approaches

>

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is a widely recognized treatment for anxiety disorders. We help you identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to anxiety, developing healthier coping strategies for long-term relief.

<

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

ACT helps you develop psychological flexibility and learn to accept difficult emotions while staying committed to your values. This approach is particularly effective for anxiety and stress management.

>

Mindfulness-Based Approaches

Learn mindfulness and relaxation techniques that help reduce anxiety symptoms in the moment and build greater emotional regulation skills for ongoing stress management.

Exposure Therapy

For specific phobias and anxiety disorders, gradual exposure therapy helps you face fears in a safe, controlled environment, reducing avoidance behaviors and building confidence.

=

Panic Disorder Treatment

Focused treatment for panic attacks and panic disorder, helping you understand the panic cycle and develop effective strategies to prevent and manage panic episodes.

=

Social Anxiety Support

Targeted interventions for social anxiety disorder, including social skills training, cognitive restructuring, and gradual exposure to social situations to build confidence.

Anxiety Conditions We Treat in London, Ontario

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Persistent worry and anxiety about multiple areas of life, affecting daily functioning and quality of life.

Panic Disorder

Recurrent panic attacks and persistent fear of having future panic attacks, often leading to avoidance behaviors.

Social Anxiety Disorder

Intense fear of social situations and being judged by others, impacting relationships and professional life.

Specific Phobias

Irrational fears of specific objects, situations, or activities that lead to avoidance and distress.

Agoraphobia

Fear of being in situations where escape might be difficult or help might not be available during a panic attack.

Health Anxiety

Excessive worry about having or developing serious medical conditions, despite medical reassurance.

Performance Anxiety

Anxiety related to performing in specific situations such as public speaking, tests, or work presentations.

Separation Anxiety

Excessive distress when separated from loved ones or familiar environments, affecting daily activities.
