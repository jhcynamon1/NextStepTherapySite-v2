Getting Started with Therapy

Common questions about beginning your mental health journey and understanding when professional support can be beneficial.

How do I know if I need therapy?

If you're experiencing persistent anxiety, depression, relationship challenges, workplace stress, or life transitions that are affecting your daily functioning, therapy can be helpful. You don't need to wait for a crisis - many people benefit from therapy as a proactive approach to mental health maintenance.

Do I need a referral to see a therapist in Ontario?

No, you do not need a referral to see a Registered Psychotherapist in Ontario. You can book directly. A referral is only required for OHIP-covered services like psychiatrists or hospital programs, which have waitlists of 6-12 months.

What is a free consultation, and what happens during it?

A free 15-minute consultation is a brief phone conversation where we discuss your current challenges, answer questions about the therapeutic process, and determine if we're a good fit. There's no pressure to commit, and it helps both of us understand if virtual therapy would be beneficial for your situation.

How quickly can I start therapy?

Most clients can schedule their first session within 1-2 weeks of initial contact, with same-week appointments often available. This is much faster than public mental health services which typically have waitlists of several months.

Virtual Therapy Process

Understanding how online counselling works and what to expect from virtual sessions across Ontario.

Is virtual therapy as effective as in-person sessions?

Research consistently shows that virtual therapy is as effective as in-person therapy for most mental health concerns, including anxiety, depression, and relationship issues. Many clients prefer the convenience and comfort of receiving therapy from their own space.

What technology do I need for virtual sessions?

You'll need a device with internet access, camera, and microphone - this can be a computer, tablet, or smartphone. We use a secure, PIPEDA-compliant platform that's simple to access and doesn't require special software downloads.

How do you ensure privacy in virtual sessions?

All sessions are conducted through encrypted, secure platforms that meet healthcare privacy standards. We recommend using a private space with headphones or earbuds to ensure confidentiality during your session.

Professional Credentials & Regulation

Understanding CRPO registration, professional standards, and what these credentials mean for your care.

What does CRPO #10979 mean?

CRPO stands for College of Registered Psychotherapists of Ontario. This registration number indicates that Jesse Cynamon is licensed to practice psychotherapy in Ontario and adheres to professional standards, ethical guidelines, and continuing education requirements.

What is the difference between a psychotherapist and a psychologist?

Both are regulated mental health professionals in Ontario. Registered Psychotherapists focus on talk therapy and counselling interventions. Psychologists may also provide therapy but can additionally conduct psychological testing and assessments. Both can effectively treat anxiety, depression, and relationship concerns.

How do I verify professional credentials?

You can verify any Ontario psychotherapist's registration through the CRPO website's public register. Simply search for the practitioner's name or registration number to confirm their current status and any disciplinary actions.

Insurance & Financial Information

Understanding costs, insurance coverage, and payment options for virtual therapy services in Ontario.

Is therapy covered by OHIP in Ontario?

No, OHIP does not cover private psychotherapy with Registered Psychotherapists. However, many extended health plans cover Registered Psychotherapist services—check your benefits for "Registered Psychotherapist" or "Psychological Services" coverage. OHIP covers psychiatrist appointments (6-12 month waitlists), hospital-based programs, and mental health care through family doctors.

How much does therapy cost in Ontario?

Therapy sessions at Next Step Therapy are $175 for a 50-minute session. Ontario therapy costs typically range from $140-$200 per session for Registered Psychotherapists. Many health insurance plans cover Registered Psychotherapist services—check with your provider to confirm coverage. I provide detailed receipts for reimbursement.

Does insurance cover virtual therapy sessions?

Yes, many extended health plans in Ontario cover Registered Psychotherapist services, including virtual sessions. Check your benefits to confirm coverage. I provide detailed receipts that work with Sun Life, Manulife, Canada Life, Green Shield, Blue Cross, and other major providers.

What if I don't have insurance coverage?

Individual therapy sessions are $175 for 50 minutes. Many clients find this investment worthwhile given the convenience of virtual sessions and the ability to access professional care quickly. Some employers offer Employee Assistance Programs (EAP) that may cover initial sessions.

Can I claim therapy on my taxes in Canada?

Yes, psychotherapy fees are eligible medical expenses on your CRA tax return if you pay out-of-pocket (amounts not reimbursed by insurance). Save your receipts showing your therapist's license number (CRPO #10979). This applies to sessions with Registered Psychotherapists, psychologists, and other regulated providers.

Do you offer sliding scale or reduced fees?

Currently, we maintain consistent pricing to ensure service sustainability. However, we encourage you to explore your extended health benefits, as many plans provide substantial coverage for psychotherapy services.

Treatment Approaches & Focus Areas

Information about therapeutic methods, treatment duration, and specific approaches for different mental health concerns.

What therapy approaches do you use?

Primary approaches include Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT). These evidence-based methods are particularly effective for anxiety, depression, workplace stress, and relationship challenges common among Ontario residents.

How long does therapy typically take?

This varies based on individual goals and concerns. Many clients notice improvements within 4-6 sessions, with significant progress typically occurring within 8-12 weeks of regular sessions. We regularly review progress and adjust treatment plans accordingly.

Can you prescribe medication?

Registered Psychotherapists cannot prescribe medication. If medication consultation is needed, we can discuss referral options to psychiatrists or encourage discussion with your family physician about your mental health concerns.

Scheduling & Logistics

Practical information about appointment scheduling, session frequency, and cancellation policies.

What are your hours of operation?

Virtual sessions are available Monday through Friday, with some evening and weekend appointments. This flexibility accommodates various work schedules and family responsibilities across Ontario's diverse communities.

What if I need to cancel or reschedule?

We require 24 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling. This allows us to offer the time slot to other clients. Last-minute cancellations may be subject to a cancellation fee.

How often should I attend therapy sessions?

Most clients benefit from weekly sessions initially, with some transitioning to bi-weekly sessions as progress is made. Session frequency is collaboratively determined based on your needs, goals, and availability.

Ontario-Specific Questions

Information about accessing virtual therapy services across different regions of Ontario and understanding local context.

Can I access services from anywhere in Ontario?

Yes, as long as you're physically located in Ontario during sessions, you can access virtual therapy services regardless of your specific city or region. This includes rural and remote communities throughout the province.

How do you understand different regional pressures across Ontario?

Having worked with clients from Toronto's urban environment to Northern Ontario's rural communities, there's awareness of how regional factors - housing costs, employment markets, social resources - impact mental health. Treatment approaches consider these contextual factors.

What about language or cultural considerations?

Sessions are conducted in English. While cultural sensitivity is prioritized in all therapeutic work, for specific cultural or linguistic needs, referrals to appropriate practitioners with specific focus may be recommended.

Emergency & Crisis Information

Important: Emergency Services Information

These services are not appropriate for mental health emergencies. If you're experiencing thoughts of self-harm, suicide, or other psychiatric emergencies, please call or text 988 (Suicide Crisis Helpline), contact 911, visit your nearest emergency department, or call the Ontario Crisis Line at 1-877-377-7775.

What if I have a mental health emergency?

Virtual therapy services are not appropriate for mental health emergencies. If you're experiencing thoughts of self-harm or suicide, please contact:

  • 988 (Suicide Crisis Helpline) - call or text 24/7
  • 911 for immediate emergency services
  • Ontario Crisis Line: 1-877-377-7775
  • Kids Help Phone: 1-800-668-6868
  • Your local hospital emergency department

What happens if I experience a crisis between sessions?

While ongoing support between sessions isn't available, crisis resources are provided during treatment. We work together to develop safety plans and identify appropriate resources for various levels of mental health concerns.
