Frequently Asked Questions - Online Counselling Ontario

How much does counselling cost in Ontario?
Online counselling in Ontario typically costs $175 per session. Many Ontario residents have extended health insurance through their employer that covers psychotherapy services, reducing or eliminating out-of-pocket costs. I provide receipts you can submit to your insurance provider. Unlike traditional in-person therapy, online counselling eliminates commuting costs and time, making professional mental health support more accessible and affordable for Ontario residents.
Is counselling covered by OHIP in Ontario?
Online counselling with a CRPO-registered psychotherapist is not covered by OHIP. However, most extended health insurance plans offered by Ontario employers cover psychotherapy services, including online counselling. Typical coverage ranges from $500-$3,000 per year. Check your benefits plan or contact your HR department to confirm your coverage for mental health services.
Can I claim counselling on insurance Ontario?
Yes! Most Ontario employers offer extended health benefits that cover psychotherapy services, including online counselling. As a CRPO-registered psychotherapist, I provide detailed receipts with all required information for insurance claims. Common insurance providers that cover online counselling in Ontario include Manulife, Sun Life, Green Shield, and Great-West Life. Simply submit your receipt through your insurance portal or mobile app for reimbursement.
What does CRPO stand for in counselling?
CRPO stands for the College of Registered Psychotherapists of Ontario. It's the regulatory body that oversees all psychotherapists practicing in Ontario. CRPO registration (#10979) means I've completed accredited education, supervised clinical hours, passed rigorous examinations, and maintain professional liability insurance. This protects Ontario residents by ensuring counselling services meet provincial standards for safety, ethics, and effectiveness.
What is the difference between counselling and therapy?
In Ontario, "counselling" and "therapy" are used interchangeably to describe professional mental health support. Both involve working with a registered professional to address anxiety, depression, stress, relationship issues, and life transitions. The term "psychotherapy" refers to evidence-based therapeutic approaches like CBT and ACT. Whether you search for "online counselling Ontario" or "therapy Ontario," you're looking for the same professional service‚Äîstructured support from a CRPO-registered practitioner.
What is the difference between psychologist and counsellor in Ontario?
In Ontario, psychologists (regulated by College of Psychologists of Ontario) typically have doctoral degrees and can conduct psychological assessments and testing. Counsellors and psychotherapists (regulated by CRPO) provide talk therapy for anxiety, depression, relationship issues, and life challenges. Both can provide effective online counselling throughout Ontario. For most mental health concerns‚Äîstress, anxiety, depression, relationship difficulties‚Äîa CRPO-registered psychotherapist offers the same evidence-based therapy at lower cost.
Are online counselling sessions effective?
Yes! Research consistently shows online counselling is as effective as in-person therapy for anxiety, depression, and stress management. Online counselling offers unique advantages for Ontario residents: no commuting (especially valuable during harsh winters), flexible scheduling for busy professionals, access to therapy from rural or Northern Ontario communities, and the comfort of receiving support from your own home. Secure video platforms ensure confidentiality while providing the same therapeutic relationship and evidence-based interventions as traditional therapy.
