Understanding Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety affects millions of Canadians and can manifest in various forms. While some anxiety is normal and even helpful, an anxiety disorder occurs when anxiety becomes overwhelming, persistent, and interferes with daily functioning.

Common anxiety symptoms include:

  • Persistent worry or fear that feels uncontrollable
  • Physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat, sweating, or trembling
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Avoidance of situations that trigger anxiety
  • Sleep disturbances or restlessness
  • Muscle tension and fatigue
  • Feeling on edge or easily startled
  • Panic attacks or intense episodes of fear

Types of Anxiety We Treat

Our anxiety therapy services in Mississauga address various anxiety disorders:

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Chronic, excessive worry about everyday situations
  • Panic Disorder: Recurring panic attacks and fear of future attacks
  • Social Anxiety Disorder: Intense fear of social situations and being judged by others
  • Specific Phobias: Intense fear of specific objects or situations
  • Health Anxiety: Excessive worry about having or developing a serious illness
  • Performance Anxiety: Fear related to work presentations, tests, or public speaking

Evidence-Based Anxiety Treatment Approaches

Our anxiety therapy in Mississauga incorporates proven therapeutic methods tailored to your specific needs:

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

CBT is the gold standard for anxiety treatment. This approach helps you identify and change negative thought patterns that contribute to anxiety. You'll learn practical skills to challenge anxious thoughts and develop more balanced thinking patterns.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

ACT teaches you to accept anxious feelings while committing to actions that align with your values. This approach is particularly effective for reducing the struggle against anxiety and building psychological flexibility.

Mindfulness-Based Interventions

Mindfulness techniques help you observe anxious thoughts and feelings without being overwhelmed by them. These practices can significantly reduce anxiety symptoms and improve overall well-being.

Exposure Therapy

For specific phobias and avoidance behaviours, gradual exposure therapy helps you face feared situations in a safe, controlled manner, reducing anxiety over time.

Frequently Asked Questions About Anxiety Therapy

How do I know if my anxiety needs professional treatment?
Consider therapy if anxiety interferes with your daily activities, work, relationships, or sleep. If you're avoiding situations due to anxiety or experiencing physical symptoms like panic attacks, professional support can be very beneficial.
Is anxiety therapy covered by insurance in Ontario?
Yes, anxiety therapy sessions with a Registered Psychotherapist are covered by most extended health insurance plans in Ontario. We can provide direct billing for many insurance providers.
How long does anxiety therapy take to work?
Many people notice some improvement within the first few sessions, with significant progress typically seen within 8-12 sessions. The duration depends on the severity of anxiety, your specific goals, and how actively you engage with the therapeutic process.
Can anxiety therapy help with panic attacks?
Yes, therapy is highly effective for panic attacks. You'll learn techniques to manage panic symptoms, understand what triggers attacks, and develop confidence in your ability to cope with and prevent future episodes.
