My Approach to Burlington Clients

I believe effective therapy meets you where you are—literally and figuratively. For Burlington clients, this often means understanding the tension between small-town community connection and big-city career demands.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) helps Burlington clients develop flexibility around things they can't control—like train delays or traffic—while taking meaningful action toward what matters most, whether that's family time, career advancement, or personal wellbeing.

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) techniques can be particularly helpful for managing anxiety about commuting, work performance, or financial pressures common in Burlington's competitive housing market.

Mindfulness approaches work well with Burlington's natural setting. We might incorporate awareness practices you can use during walks along the waterfront trail or while sitting in your backyard garden.

I typically suggest we start with weekly sessions to build momentum and address immediate concerns. As you develop coping strategies and gain insight, we can adjust frequency based on your needs and schedule.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is online therapy as effective as meeting in person?
Research consistently shows online therapy produces equivalent results to in-person treatment for anxiety, depression, and most other concerns. Many clients find they feel more comfortable sharing personal information from their own environment, leading to deeper therapeutic work.
What technology do I need for virtual therapy?
Any device with internet and a camera—computer, tablet, or smartphone. Our platform works through your web browser with no downloads required. We'll do a quick technology check during your free consultation to ensure everything works smoothly.
How do you understand Burlington-specific concerns?
I work with many clients from Burlington and the broader Halton region. I understand local pressures like housing costs, commuting stress on the QEW and GO trains, seasonal changes by the lake, and the balance between community involvement and privacy in a smaller city.
What if my internet connection is unreliable?
Burlington generally has good internet infrastructure, but technical issues occasionally happen. We always have backup plans—whether switching to phone, rescheduling, or using mobile data. Stable connection helps, but perfection isn't required.
Can I take sessions while my family is home?
Privacy is essential for effective therapy. We'll discuss strategies for ensuring uninterrupted time, whether that means scheduling during school hours, using headphones, asking family members to go for a walk, or finding other solutions that work for your Burlington household.
How quickly can I start online therapy?
I typically have availability for new online therapy clients within the same week. Burlington residents often appreciate the convenience of not having to coordinate appointment times with downtown Toronto traffic or GO train schedules.
Is my virtual therapy session confidential?
Absolutely. Our platform uses bank-level encryption and is PHIPA compliant. As a Registered Psychotherapist, all sessions are governed by strict confidentiality standards—the same privacy protections apply whether we meet virtually or in person.
Do you accept insurance for virtual therapy?
Yes, most extended health insurance plans that cover "Registered Psychotherapist" services will cover virtual therapy sessions. I provide detailed receipts for all insurance claims. Session fee is $175 per 50-minute session.
What happens in an emergency during online therapy?
Online therapy works best for individuals who aren't experiencing active crisis. We'll create a safety plan that includes local Burlington resources, emergency contacts, and clear protocols. For immediate emergencies, always call 911 or go to Burlington's Joseph Brant Hospital emergency department.
Can you accommodate shift workers or irregular schedules?
Absolutely. Many Burlington residents work non-traditional hours, travel for work, or have varying schedules due to GO train commuting. I offer flexible scheduling including early mornings, evenings, and weekends to accommodate your lifestyle.
