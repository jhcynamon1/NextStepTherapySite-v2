What Is Online Therapy?

Online therapy (also called virtual therapy or teletherapy) is mental health treatment delivered through secure video conferencing. You meet with a CRPO-registered psychotherapist via video call from your home, office, or any private location with internet access. Sessions are the same length and follow the same evidence-based approaches as in-person therapy—the only difference is the delivery format.

For Burlington residents, online therapy eliminates the need to commute to a therapist's office, making it easier to fit mental health support into a busy schedule.

Why Burlington Residents Choose Online Therapy

Living in Burlington often means balancing suburban life with Toronto-area work commitments. Between the GO train, QEW traffic, and packed schedules, adding another appointment can feel overwhelming.

Benefits of Online Therapy for Burlington:

  • No Commute Required: Save 30-60 minutes you'd spend driving to an office. Do therapy from home after work.
  • Flexible Scheduling: Evening and lunch-hour appointments available. Therapy doesn't have to compete with your workday.
  • Same Quality Care: Research shows online therapy is equally effective as in-person for anxiety, depression, and stress.
  • Privacy From Home: No chance of running into someone you know in a waiting room.

How Online Therapy Works

Getting started with online therapy is straightforward:

  1. Book a Free Consultation: Schedule a 15-minute video call to discuss what you're looking for and see if we're a good fit.
  2. Schedule Your First Session: If we decide to work together, we'll book your first 50-minute session—often within the same week.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is online therapy as effective as in-person therapy?

Yes, research consistently shows that online therapy is equally effective as in-person therapy for most mental health concerns, including anxiety, depression, and stress. A comprehensive review in the Journal of Medical Internet Research found no significant outcome differences between online and in-person formats. Many Burlington residents find online therapy more convenient and sustainable long-term.

How much does online therapy cost in Burlington?

Online therapy sessions are $175 per 50-minute session. Most extended health benefit plans in Ontario cover psychotherapy with a CRPO-registered therapist, typically covering 50-100% of the session cost. Insurance receipts are provided after each session for reimbursement. Check your benefits under 'psychotherapy' or 'mental health practitioners' for your coverage limits.

What do I need for online therapy?

You need a computer, tablet, or smartphone with a camera and microphone, a stable internet connection, and a private space where you can talk freely. Many Burlington clients do sessions from their home office, bedroom, or even their parked car during a lunch break. All you need is privacy and an internet connection.

Is online therapy secure and confidential?

Yes, all online sessions use encrypted, PIPEDA-compliant video platforms designed for healthcare. As a CRPO-registered psychotherapist (#10979), I'm bound by strict confidentiality requirements. Your sessions remain private, and all records are protected according to Ontario healthcare privacy standards.

Do I need a referral for online therapy?

No, you don't need a referral to see a Registered Psychotherapist in Ontario. You can book directly by scheduling a free 15-minute consultation. Some insurance plans may require a referral for coverage, so check your specific plan details.

What issues can online therapy help with?

Online therapy is effective for a wide range of concerns including anxiety, depression, workplace stress and burnout, life transitions, relationship issues, and more. The format works well for most mental health concerns, with the added convenience of not having to commute to appointments.
