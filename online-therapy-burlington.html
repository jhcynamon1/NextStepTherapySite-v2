Living in Burlington offers the best of both worlds—scenic lakefront beauty with proximity to Toronto's opportunities. But this unique position also brings distinct challenges. Many residents find themselves juggling demanding commutes, competitive work environments, and the pressure to maintain a balanced lifestyle in a rapidly growing community.

  • Commuter Burnout: Daily drives or GO Train rides to Toronto drain your energy before you even start working
  • Work-Life Balance Struggles: Living in a "bedroom community" makes it hard to truly disconnect from work
  • Social Isolation: Many friends work in Toronto, making it difficult to maintain local connections
  • Financial Pressure: Burlington's rising costs create stress about housing, lifestyle, and future planning
  • Time Scarcity: Between commuting and work, there's little time for self-care or pursuing interests

If several of these experiences resonate, you're not alone. Many Burlington residents face similar challenges as they navigate the unique demands of living in a commuter community while maintaining personal well-being and meaningful relationships.

How Online Therapy Works

If you've never done virtual therapy before, it's simpler than you might think. If you can video chat with family or friends, you can do online therapy. The technology is straightforward, and the focus remains on the conversation and connection between us.

Getting Started is Simple:

1. Free Consultation Call (15 minutes)

We'll have a brief phone conversation where you can share what you're experiencing and ask any questions about therapy. This helps us both determine if we're a good fit.

2. Schedule Your First Session

If we decide to work together, we'll find a time that works with your schedule. I offer sessions throughout the day and evening, including some weekend slots.

3. Simple Technical Setup

I'll send you a secure link for our video session. All you need is a device with a camera and microphone—your laptop, tablet, or smartphone works perfectly.

4. Create Your Space

Find a private, comfortable spot in your home where you can talk openly. Many people use their bedroom, home office, or a quiet corner of their living room.

What to Expect in Sessions:

Our 50-minute sessions will be conversational and collaborative. I use evidence-based approaches like Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), which focuses on helping you develop psychological flexibility and move toward what matters most to you.

Practical Focus

We'll work on strategies you can actually use in your daily life in Burlington—realistic and sustainable approaches.

Your Pace

Some people feel better after a few sessions, others prefer longer-term support. We'll go at whatever pace feels right for you.

Between Sessions

You can email with questions or observations. Supporting your growth between sessions is part of the process.

No Long Commitments

We'll work together as long as it's helpful, whether that's a few sessions or several months.

Frequently Asked Questions

"Is virtual therapy as effective as in-person?"

Research consistently shows that virtual therapy is just as effective as in-person therapy for most concerns. Many Burlington residents actually prefer it because they're in their own comfortable space and don't have to add commute stress to their day.

"What if my internet connection isn't perfect?"

Most home internet connections in Burlington are perfectly adequate for video therapy. If we experience technical difficulties, we can always switch to a phone call for that session.

"How do I know if I need therapy?"

If stress, anxiety, low mood, or life challenges are affecting your daily functioning, relationships, or overall well-being, therapy can be helpful. You don't need to wait for a crisis—early support often prevents bigger problems later.

"What if someone in my household overhears?"

Many people find a private space in their bedroom, home office, or even sit in their car in the driveway. We can discuss strategies for ensuring privacy during your consultation call.

"How long will I need therapy?"

This varies by person and situation. Some people feel significantly better after a few sessions, while others prefer longer-term support. We'll regularly check in about your progress and adjust as needed.

"Can I use my work benefits?"

Yes, most employee extended health plans cover Registered Psychotherapist services. I provide receipts that are easy to submit to your insurance company or for reimbursement.
