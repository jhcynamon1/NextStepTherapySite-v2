Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I need therapy for a life transition vs. just time to adjust?
If you're feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or unable to make decisions about your transition after several weeks, therapy can help. Also consider therapy if the transition is affecting your sleep, relationships, work performance, or if you're using unhealthy coping strategies. Sometimes having professional support early in a transition actually shortens the adjustment period.
What's the difference between life coaching and therapy for transitions?
Therapy addresses both the emotional and practical aspects of transitions. While coaching focuses on goals and action steps, therapy also helps process grief, anxiety, identity questions, and past patterns that might interfere with moving forward. As a registered psychotherapist, I can address mental health concerns that often arise during major life changes.
Can you help if my transition was forced by circumstances beyond my control?
Absolutely. Unexpected job loss, divorce, illness, or other involuntary changes often bring additional layers of grief, anger, and helplessness. I help you process these difficult emotions while finding ways to reclaim some sense of control and direction. Often these forced transitions, while painful, can lead to unexpected growth and new opportunities.
How do you approach career transitions for people over 40 in Ontario?
Career changes later in life have unique considerationsâ€”financial responsibilities, family obligations, age discrimination concerns, and questions about starting over. I help you realistically assess your options while considering Ontario's employment landscape, potential retraining programs, and how to leverage your existing experience in new ways.
Do you help with transitions related to aging parents and caregiving?
Yes, this is increasingly common in Ontario as our population ages. I help adult children navigate the emotional complexity of watching parents decline, making difficult decisions about care, managing family dynamics around caregiving, and balancing your own life while supporting aging parents. This includes practical guidance about Ontario's healthcare and support systems.
How does online therapy work for life transitions support?
Online therapy is particularly effective for transition support because it's flexible and accessible during already stressful times. We use secure video platforms, and I can provide resources digitally. Many clients appreciate having sessions from home during vulnerable periods. I'm licensed to provide online therapy to anyone in Ontario, so location isn't a barrier.
What if I'm not sure what I want after my transition?
Not knowing what you want is completely normal and often the first thing we work on. Many people know what they don't want anymore but haven't figured out what they do want. Through values clarification, exploring your history of what's brought meaning and satisfaction, and experimenting with small changes, we gradually discover your direction together.
How long does therapy for life transitions typically take?
It varies greatly depending on the complexity of your transition and what support you need. Some clients come for crisis support during acute transitions (6-10 sessions), others want ongoing support throughout a longer transition process (6 months to a year), and some use therapy intermittently as new challenges arise. We'll regularly check in about your goals and adjust as needed.
