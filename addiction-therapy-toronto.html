Understanding Addiction

Addiction is a chronic condition characterized by compulsive engagement in rewarding stimuli despite harmful consequences. It affects the brain's reward, motivation, and memory functions, making it difficult to stop without professional support.

Signs of addiction may include:

  • Inability to control or reduce use despite wanting to
  • Continued use despite negative consequences
  • Tolerance requiring increased amounts for the same effect
  • Withdrawal symptoms when use is stopped
  • Neglecting responsibilities, relationships, or self-care
  • Unsuccessful attempts to quit or control use
  • Spending significant time obtaining, using, or recovering from use
  • Giving up important activities due to addiction

Types of Addiction We Address

  • Substance Use Disorders: Alcohol, drugs, prescription medications
  • Behavioural Addictions: Gambling, gaming, internet, shopping, food
  • Dual Diagnosis: Addiction co-occurring with mental health conditions
  • Process Addictions: Work, sex, relationships, exercise
  • Nicotine Dependence: Smoking and tobacco cessation support

Evidence-Based Addiction Treatment Approaches

Motivational Interviewing (MI)

MI helps you explore and resolve ambivalence about change. This collaborative approach strengthens your own motivation and commitment to recovery without judgment or confrontation.

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps identify and change thought patterns and behaviours that contribute to addictive behaviours. You'll learn coping strategies and skills to manage triggers and cravings.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

ACT focuses on accepting difficult emotions and urges while committing to actions aligned with your values, rather than using substances or behaviours to avoid discomfort.

Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention

Combining mindfulness meditation with relapse prevention strategies to increase awareness of triggers and develop healthier responses to cravings and stress.

Frequently Asked Questions About Addiction Therapy

Do I need to be ready to quit to start addiction therapy?
No, you don't need to be ready to quit to benefit from addiction therapy. We can work together to explore your relationship with substances or behaviours and help you determine what changes, if any, you want to make.
Is addiction therapy covered by insurance in Ontario?
Yes, addiction therapy sessions with a Registered Psychotherapist are covered by most extended health insurance plans in Ontario. We can provide direct billing for many insurance providers.
Will my addiction therapy sessions be confidential?
Yes, all therapy sessions are completely confidential. Information is only shared with your consent or in rare situations where there's immediate risk of harm, as outlined in professional ethics guidelines.
Can therapy help if I've tried to quit before and failed?
Absolutely. Previous unsuccessful attempts don't mean therapy won't work. Each attempt provides valuable information about what works and what doesn't, helping us develop more effective strategies.
Do you work with people who aren't ready for complete abstinence?
Yes, we support various recovery goals including harm reduction approaches for those who aren't ready or able to completely abstain from substance use.
