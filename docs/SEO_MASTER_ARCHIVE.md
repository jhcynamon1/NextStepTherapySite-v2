<a name="research--learnings"></a>
## üß† **5. RESEARCH & LEARNINGS**

### **CRITICAL STRATEGIC LEARNINGS**

#### **‚ùå LEARNING #1: Content Length Misconception (CORRECTED)**

**Previous Approach (WRONG):**
- Copied B2B mechanic model (Diesel Dudes) = 1,200 words
- OR created educational encyclopedia pages = 8,000 words
- Thought: Shorter = more transactional like mechanic services

**Corrected Understanding:**
- **Optimal Range: 2,500-4,000 words** for therapy service pages
- **Why:** Therapy = hybrid informational-transactional intent
  - Users need education to build trust (unlike mechanics - pure transactional)
  - But they want to take action (unlike pure research - informational)
- **Sources:** 
  - Perplexity AI research on therapy search intent
  - Firecrawl competitor analysis (Inner Oak: 500-800 words per condition, Brookhaven: 2,000-3,000)
  - Diesel Dudes: 1,200 works for mechanics, NOT therapy

**Evidence:**
- Diesel Dudes (mechanics): 1,200 words, ZERO FAQs, local elements focus
- Inner Oak (therapy): 500-800 words per condition (short!), but MANY pages
- Our pages: 8,000 words = too educational, dilutes conversion focus
- Sweet spot: 2,500-4,000 words + clear CTAs throughout

---

#### **‚ùå LEARNING #2: Therapy Search Intent Misunderstood (CORRECTED)**

**Previous Misunderstanding:**
- Therapy = same as mechanic/lawyer (pure transactional)
- Ahrefs says "informational" = wrong keyword target

**Corrected Understanding:**
- Therapy searches are **"hybrid informational-transactional"**
- Users research symptoms, treatment options (informational phase)
- But ultimate goal is booking appointment (transactional phase)
- Need content that builds trust AND provides clear conversion paths
- Ahrefs intent classification can be misleading - trust GSC data instead

**How This Changed Strategy:**
- Can't just copy Diesel Dudes' short transactional pages (too little trust-building)
- Can't do pure informational content (users won't convert)
- Need 2,500-4,000 words to demonstrate E-E-A-T and build trust
- But maintain action-oriented CTAs throughout
- Balance education with conversion opportunities

---

#### **‚úÖ LEARNING #3: Multi-Service Hub Strategy (Inner Oak Model)**

**Discovery via Firecrawl:**
- Burlington's #1 competitor (Inner Oak) uses specific strategy:
  - Homepage = general multi-service hub
  - **Separate dedicated pages for EACH condition** (anxiety, depression, ADHD, BPD, PTSD, etc.)
  - Each condition page = 500-800 words (shorter than expected!)
  - Clear differentiation by mental health condition

**Why This Works:**
- Users search by condition ("anxiety therapy burlington" not "therapy burlington")
- Separate pages prevent keyword cannibalization
- Hub-spoke internal linking signals topical authority
- Comprehensive coverage beats generic coverage

**Applied to Next Step Therapy:**
- Created: burlington-online-therapy.html (multi-service hub)
- Created: anxiety-therapy-burlington.html (anxiety-specific spoke)
- Created: depression-therapy-burlington.html (depression-specific spoke)
- Next: workplace-stress, ADHD, trauma pages (more spokes)
- Result: Hub-spoke model prevents cannibalization, builds authority

---

#### **‚úÖ LEARNING #4: Keyword Cannibalization Detection Process**

**Process Developed:**
1. **GSC Query Analysis:** Export top queries per page
2. **Overlap Identification:** Find queries appearing on multiple pages
3. **Volume Assessment:** Calculate total impressions being split
4. **Intent Mapping:** Determine if pages should target different intents
5. **Consolidation Decision:** Merge duplicates OR differentiate focus

**Burlington Example:**
- Query: "online therapy burlington" (2.4K volume)
- Appearing on:
  - virtual-therapy-burlington.html (465 impressions)
  - burlington-online-therapy.html (195 impressions)
  - anxiety-therapy-burlington.html (242 impressions - WRONG!)
- **Total waste:** 660 impressions split between pages targeting same keyword
- **Solution:** 
  - Redirect #1 (virtual ‚Üí online therapy hub)
  - Enhance #2 (online therapy hub targets "online therapy burlington")
  - Refocus #3 (anxiety page targets "anxiety therapy burlington" instead)

**Result:**
- Clear keyword targeting per page
- No more cannibalization
- Authority consolidated instead of diluted

### **COMPETITOR RESEARCH INSIGHTS**

#### **Diesel Dudes (Mechanics - Different Industry):**
**Firecrawled:** columbia-sc and charlotte-nc pages

**Findings:**
- ~1,200 words per page
- ZERO FAQs (our documentation was WRONG about this!)
- 11 H2 sections, 15+ H3 subsections
- Hyper-local content (specific highways, landmarks, businesses)
- 3 internal links to nearby city pages

**What We Learned:**
- Local elements matter more than word count
- But 1,200 words TOO SHORT for therapy (trust-building needed)
- FAQs help therapy (featured snippets, E-E-A-T) even though Diesel Dudes doesn't use them
- Hub-spoke city linking works

---

#### **Inner Oak (Burlington #1 Therapy Competitor):**
**Firecrawled:** Homepage + anxiety + depression pages

**Findings:**
- Homepage: Multi-service hub listing all services
- **8+ separate condition pages** (anxiety, depression, ADHD, BPD, PTSD, etc.)
- Each page: 500-800 words (SHORT!)
- Services listed: Various age groups, in-person + online
- Clear differentiation by condition

**What We Learned:**
- Separate page per condition = winning strategy
- Shorter pages work IF you have comprehensive coverage
- Multi-service hub + condition spokes = ideal structure
- They dominate Burlington because they cover MORE conditions (not longer content)

**Our Gap:**
- Inner Oak: 8+ condition pages
- Us (Nov 4): 3 condition pages (anxiety, depression, online therapy hub)
- **Action:** Need 5-8 more condition pages for Burlington (stress, ADHD, trauma, etc.)

---

#### **Brookhaven Psychotherapy (Toronto Competitor):**
**Firecrawled:** Homepage

**Findings:**
- Multi-service hub similar to Inner Oak
- In-person + online therapy
- Services for various age groups
- "What we can help with" section listing many conditions

**What We Learned:**
- Multi-service hub model is standard in therapy
- Comprehensive service listing builds trust
- Age group differentiation (children, teens, adults, seniors)

### **PERPLEXITY AI RESEARCH INSIGHTS**

**Question:** What is therapy search intent?

**Answer:** Hybrid informational-transactional
- Users need information to build trust (credentials, approach, process)
- But ultimate goal is transactional (book appointment)
- Different from mechanics (pure transactional) or research (pure informational)
- Requires content that educates AND converts

**Impact on Strategy:**
- 2,500-4,000 words = optimal balance
- Include credentials, approach, FAQs (informational)
- Multiple CTAs throughout (transactional)
- Balance education with conversion opportunities
