## The Reality Check

### Two Major Constraints

1. **SAB (Service Area Business):** No physical address = 10-20x harder to rank in map pack
2. **CRPO Standard 6.2.2:** Cannot solicit reviews from clients

### What This Means

The popular "PROOF" system recommends "6 review requests per job" - **this is NOT compliant for Ontario RPs**.

**CRPO Standard 6.2.2 states:**
> "Registrants do not request or solicit testimonials or use them in their advertising."

**What's Prohibited:**
- ❌ Asking clients for reviews (even politely)
- ❌ Sending review request emails to clients
- ❌ Including "Leave us a review" in client communications
- ❌ Using testimonials on your website
- ❌ Promoting your star rating in advertising
- ❌ Influencing which reviews get published

**What's Allowed:**
- ✅ Clients can leave reviews voluntarily (you just can't ask)
- ✅ Non-clients (friends, family, colleagues) can be asked
- ✅ Organic reviews that happen naturally
- ✅ Responding to reviews (without soliciting more)

## The Compliant Review Strategy

### Who CAN You Ask?

| Person Type | Can Ask? | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Current/former clients | ❌ NO | CRPO prohibits solicitation |
| Friends who know your work | ✅ YES | They're not clients |
| Family members | ✅ YES | They're not clients |
| Professional colleagues | ⚠️ GREY | Could be seen as "endorsement" - careful |
| People who attended workshops/talks | ⚠️ GREY | Depends if therapeutic relationship existed |

### Realistic Review Targets

Given CRPO constraints:
- **Current:** 3 reviews
- **6-month target:** 6-10 reviews (organic + friends/family)
- **12-month target:** 10-15 reviews

This is slower than competitors but compliant.

### Maximizing Organic Reviews

You can't ask, but you CAN create conditions where satisfied clients might think to leave a review:

1. **Excellent service** - Happy clients sometimes review on their own
2. **Easy to find on Google** - When clients search for you, the review prompt appears
3. **Professional closure** - Ending therapy well leaves positive lasting impression
4. **Respond to existing reviews** - Shows you're engaged (may prompt others)

### Friends & Family Campaign (Compliant)

**Target:** 5-10 people who:
- Know your professional work
- Have seen you speak publicly
- Understand your approach
- Are NOT current or former clients

**Script for non-clients:**
> "Hey [Name], I'm working on building my Google Business Profile for my therapy practice. Since you've seen my work at [event/context], would you be willing to leave a review sharing your impression? It would really help my practice become more visible."

### Friends & Family Outreach

**Target List (Non-Clients Only):**

Think of people who:
- Attended a talk or workshop you gave
- Know you professionally but never received therapy
- Are colleagues in adjacent fields
- Are friends who understand your work

**Outreach Template:**

**SEO Tip (from @bloggersarvesh 2-4-6-8-0 Rule):**
When friends/family agree to review, suggest they naturally mention:
- **Location:** "Ontario", "Burlington", "Toronto", etc.
- **Service type:** "anxiety therapy", "virtual therapy", "stress support"
- **Your credentials:** "registered psychotherapist"

Example guidance: "If you could mention that you know me as a virtual therapist in Ontario who works with anxiety, that would help people find my practice!"

*Note: This guidance is ONLY appropriate for friends/family (non-clients). Never coach actual clients on review content per CRPO 6.2.2.*

**Target:** 5-10 people over 3 months

### Organic Review Optimization

Make it easy for clients who CHOOSE to review:

1. **Google search visibility** - When clients Google you, review prompt appears
2. **Professional ending to therapy** - Clients who feel good about closure may review
3. **Respond to all reviews** - Shows engagement, may prompt others
