## üö® SECTION 6: AVOIDING SUSPENSION

**Google is STRICT about GBP guidelines. Follow these rules religiously.**

### 6.1 CRITICAL SUSPENSION TRIGGERS

**‚ùå NEVER DO THESE:**

1. **Use same phone number twice**
   - Each business = unique phone number
   - Duplicate numbers = spam signal

2. **Buy fake reviews**
   - Google detects this easily
   - Results in suspension and review removal

3. **Pay for CTR (click-through rate) services**
   - Services on Fiverr that artificially boost clicks
   - Google detects and penalizes

4. **Use same email for multiple GBPs**
   - Each business needs unique email
   - Especially for different locations

5. **Make too many changes at once**
   - **MAXIMUM: 1 change per day**
   - Rapid changes look suspicious

6. **Change business name or address frequently**
   - Only change when absolutely necessary
   - Have documentation ready

7. **Keyword stuff business name**
   - "Next Step Therapy - Best Anxiety Therapist Toronto" = suspension
   - Keep name exactly as legal business name

8. **Use inaccurate categories**
   - Only select categories you actually provide
   - Don't add irrelevant categories for visibility

9. **Create multiple listings**
   - Only ONE listing per business
   - Duplicates = instant suspension risk

10. **Use home address or P.O. Box**
    - (You're already doing SAB correctly, but never add these)

---

### 6.2 SUSPENSION PREVENTION BEST PRACTICES

**‚úÖ DO THESE:**

1. **Keep information consistent**
   - NAP must match everywhere exactly
   - Website, GBP, citations all identical

2. **Make gradual changes**
   - Only 1 update per day maximum
   - Space out significant changes

3. **Use real business phone number**
   - Not personal cell
   - Not shared with other businesses
   - Ideally VoIP business line

4. **Keep business name clean**
   - No keywords
   - No locations
   - Exactly as on legal documents

5. **Earn reviews organically**
   - From real clients only
   - Natural pace (3-4/month is safe)
   - Varied reviewers (not all same day/location)

6. **Select accurate categories**
   - Only services you actually provide
   - Research competitor categories for ideas
   - Don't overstuff

7. **Maintain active profile**
   - Weekly posts
   - Respond to reviews
   - Update Q&A
   - Shows legitimate business activity

8. **Have documentation ready**
   - Business registration
   - Professional license (CRPO)
   - Utility bills (if you get address later)
   - Proof of legitimacy

### üö® CRPO COMPLIANCE FOR PLATFORM MODEL

**Critical Considerations:**

1. **Can't Misrepresent Credentials**
   - If a page says "Jesse Cynamon" provides therapy, Jesse must see that client

   - Solution: Each therapist lists their own CRPO number

2. **Can't Appear to Share Clients**
   - CRPO views client relationships as individual
   - Can't have one GBP where multiple therapists see clients interchangeably
   - Solution: Clear intake process where client chooses their therapist

3. **Referral Fees Need Disclosure**
   - If you're charging therapists for leads, this may need disclosure
   - CRPO guidelines on referral relationships
   - Solution: Position as "marketing service" not "referral fee"

4. **Professional Boundaries**
   - Each therapist maintains therapeutic relationship
   - You don't supervise their clinical work
   - Solution: Service agreement clarifies you provide leads, they provide therapy

**Recommended Legal Structure:**
Consult with lawyer familiar with CRPO to determine:
- Professional corporation vs. business corporation
- Service agreements with therapist clients
- Liability and malpractice insurance considerations
- Advertising and marketing compliance
