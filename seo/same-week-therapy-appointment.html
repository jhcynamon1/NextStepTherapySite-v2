What Happens in Your First Session This Week

Knowing what to expect can ease first-session anxiety. Here's how we'll use our time together to provide maximum benefit:

Your 50-Minute Session Structure:

Opening (10 minutes)

Brief introductions and establishing comfort. Clarifying confidentiality and boundaries. Understanding what brought you here this week. Setting intentions for our time together.

Exploration (25 minutes)

You share your story at your own pace. We identify key concerns and patterns. I ask clarifying questions to understand fully. This is your time to be heard without judgment.

Intervention (10 minutes)

Initial strategies for immediate relief. Coping tools you can use right away. Reframing that provides new perspective. Practical steps for the coming week.

Planning (5 minutes)

Discussing whether ongoing therapy would help. Scheduling next session if desired. Resources for between sessions. Clear action items to take forward.

Every session is tailored to your needs - this structure flexes based on what serves you best.

Insurance Coverage for Quick Access Therapy

Don't let insurance questions delay getting help. Most extended health benefits in Ontario cover our services, and we make the reimbursement process simple.

Coverage Information:

What's Typically Covered

Registered Psychotherapist services (CRPO #10979) are covered by most major insurers. Coverage usually ranges from $500-5000 annually. Many plans don't require doctor referrals for mental health. Same-week appointments are treated identically to any other therapy session.

Direct Billing Process

We provide detailed receipts immediately after each session. All required information for insurance claims included. Submit directly to your insurer for reimbursement. Most claims processed within 1-2 weeks.

Common Ontario Coverage

Manulife, Sun Life, Great-West Life, and other major providers typically cover our services. Employee Assistance Programs (EAP) often provide immediate coverage. Ontario Health Insurance Plan (OHIP) doesn't cover, but most workplace benefits do. Check your benefits booklet for "Registered Psychotherapist" coverage.

Making It Affordable

Don't let cost concerns prevent you from getting help this week. We can discuss payment options if needed. Your mental health is an investment that pays dividends. The cost of not getting help often exceeds the price of therapy.
