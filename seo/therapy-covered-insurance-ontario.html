How to Check Your Therapy Coverage

Finding out if your therapy is covered is simpler than you might think. Here's exactly how to verify your coverage in minutes:

Step 1: Locate Your Benefits Information

Through Your Employer

Check your employee benefits booklet (often available online through HR portal). Look for "Paramedical Services" or "Mental Health Services" section. Find "Registered Psychotherapist" or "Psychotherapy" in the coverage list. Note the annual maximum and per-session limits if any.

Through Your Insurance Provider

Log into your insurance company's website or app. Navigate to "Coverage" or "Benefits" section. Search for "Psychotherapy" or "Mental Health". Call the number on your benefits card for instant verification.

Step 2: Key Questions to Ask

  • "Is Registered Psychotherapist covered under my plan?"
  • "What is my annual maximum for psychotherapy?"
  • "Is there a per-visit maximum?"
  • "Do I need a referral?"
  • "Are virtual sessions covered?"
  • "What percentage is reimbursed?"

Step 3: Understanding Your Coverage

Most Ontario plans cover 80-100% of therapy costs up to the annual maximum. Coverage typically resets January 1st - don't lose unused benefits! Some plans have per-visit caps (e.g., $150/session) but our fees align with standard coverage limits.

Understanding Coverage Limitations

While most therapy services are covered, it's important to understand what typically isn't covered by insurance in Ontario:

What's Usually NOT Covered:

OHIP (Provincial Health Insurance)

OHIP doesn't cover Registered Psychotherapists. OHIP only covers psychiatrists (MD) and some psychologists in hospital settings. This is why extended health benefits are valuable. Most working Ontarians have coverage through employer benefits.

Couples or Family Therapy

Some plans only cover individual therapy. Check if your plan includes "family therapy" or "couples counselling". May need to bill as individual therapy for primary client. We can advise on best approach for your plan.

Missed Appointments

Insurance doesn't cover no-shows or late cancellations. 24-hour cancellation policy protects your benefits. Rescheduling is always preferable to losing coverage.

Annual Maximum Exceeded

Once you've used your annual allocation, additional sessions are out-of-pocket. Benefits typically reset January 1st. Some employers offer top-up coverage or HSA (Health Spending Accounts). Therapy costs may be tax-deductible medical expenses.

Maximize Your Therapy Insurance Benefits

Smart strategies to get the most from your mental health coverage:

Year-End Planning:

Use It or Lose It

Benefits don't carry over - December 31st they're gone. Start therapy in fall to use current year's benefits. Book January appointments to access new year's allocation immediately. Many clients do intensive work in Q4 to maximize coverage.

Coordinate Family Benefits

If spouse has benefits, you might have double coverage. Can claim balance from spouse's plan after yours is maxed. Children often covered under parental benefits. Maximize total family mental health support.

Combine Coverage Sources

Use EAP sessions first (they're separate from extended health). Switch to extended health benefits after EAP is exhausted. If you have HSA (Health Spending Account), use for amounts over coverage. Strategic sequencing maximizes total coverage.

Tax Benefits

Therapy expenses exceeding insurance coverage may be tax-deductible. Keep all receipts for tax filing. Medical expenses can reduce taxable income. Consult your accountant about maximizing deductions.

Insurance Coverage FAQs

Do I need a doctor's referral for insurance coverage?

No, Registered Psychotherapists in Ontario don't require referrals. You can book directly and insurance will cover it. This is different from psychiatrists who need referrals.

What's the difference between psychologist and psychotherapist coverage?

Insurance typically covers both, though sometimes under different categories or maximums. Registered Psychotherapists (RP) like Jesse are fully covered. The quality of therapy is equivalent - the difference is mainly in training path.

Can I use my spouse's insurance if mine runs out?

Yes, if your spouse has coverage, you can submit the balance to their plan after yours is exhausted. This is called coordination of benefits and can significantly extend your coverage.

Are virtual therapy sessions covered the same as in-person?

Yes, virtual therapy has identical insurance coverage to in-person sessions. This has been confirmed by all major insurers, especially since 2020.

What if my plan doesn't specifically list "Registered Psychotherapist"?

Look for "psychotherapy," "mental health services," or "paramedical services." Call your insurer - CRPO members are recognized across Ontario. Most plans updated their coverage terms recently.

How quickly will I be reimbursed?

Most insurers process claims within 5-10 business days. Electronic submission is fastest. Direct deposit speeds up payment. Paper claims may take 2-3 weeks.

What if I don't have insurance coverage?

We understand not everyone has benefits. Therapy costs may be tax-deductible medical expenses. Payment plans can be discussed. Your mental health is still important and we can explore options.
