What Is Decision Fatigue?

Decision fatigue is the deterioration of decision-making quality that occurs after a prolonged period of making choices, leading to impaired judgment, avoidance, or impulsive decisions. Research shows humans make approximately 35,000 decisions daily, and each one depletes finite cognitive resources. Unlike physical fatigue, decision fatigue often goes unrecognized‚Äîpeople attribute poor choices to character flaws rather than depleted mental capacity. Evidence-based therapy using ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy) helps professionals develop sustainable decision-making systems and "unhook" from overthinking patterns.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is decision fatigue?

Here's what I've seen: decision fatigue shows up when your brain has made so many choices that even simple ones feel impossible. You're not losing your edge‚Äîyou're depleted. Research shows we make roughly 35,000 decisions daily, and by late afternoon, most people are running on fumes. How does therapy help with decision fatigue?

We work on two things: first, unhooking from the overthinking spiral‚Äîthat loop where you analyze every angle but still can't pull the trigger.
