TL;DR - Quick Answer

Is therapy free in Canada? No, most therapy is NOT free, but here's what you need to know:

  • OHIP doesn't cover private psychotherapy sessions
  • Employer insurance typically covers $500-$3,000/year for therapy
  • Free options exist: Community mental health centers, crisis services, and hospital programs
  • Affordable alternatives: Sliding scale fees ($60-$120/session), student therapists, and group therapy
  • Average cost without insurance: $150-$225 per individual therapy session in Ontario

Is Therapy Covered by OHIP?

The Short Answer: No. OHIP does not currently cover private psychotherapy sessions with Registered Psychotherapists (RPs). Psychotherapy regulation in Ontario is relatively recent (CRPO established in 2015), and it hasn't been integrated into provincial funding yet.

What OHIP Does Cover (No Cost to You):

  • Family doctor appointments for mental health concerns (diagnosis, medication, referrals)
  • Psychiatrist consultations (medical doctors who focus on diagnosis/medication)
  • Emergency psychiatric care in hospitals
  • Some hospital-based programs (group therapy, day programs)

Why Does Therapy Cost $150-$200?

It can feel steep, but the fee covers much more than just the 50 minutes you spend in session. Here is where the money goes:

Expense Why It Matters
Education & Training Graduate degrees and ongoing clinical training to maintain CRPO license.
Liability Insurance Mandatory coverage to protect clients and the therapist.
Supervision Regular consultation with senior therapists to ensure high-quality care.
Unpaid Admin Time For every hour of therapy, there is typically 30-60 mins of unpaid notes, planning, and admin.

After expenses and taxes, therapists typically take home 40-60% of the session fee.

Free Therapy Options in Ontario

If private therapy isn't in your budget right now, use these government-funded resources:

1. ConnexOntario (Immediate Support)

  • Phone: 1-866-531-2600 (24/7, Free)
  • Best for: Crisis support, referrals to addiction services, and finding local mental health agencies.

2. BounceBack Ontario

  • What it is: A free skill-building program managed by CMHA.
  • Format: Telephone coaching and workbooks for mild-to-moderate anxiety/depression.
  • Access: Referral from family doctor or self-referral.

3. Family Health Teams (FHT)

  • How to access: Ask your family doctor if they are part of a Family Health Team. Many have social workers who provide 6-8 free sessions.

How to Check Your Insurance Benefits

Open your insurance portal or benefits booklet and look for these keywords:

  • Registered Psychotherapist (This is the exact title we hold)
  • Psychological Services (Sometimes covers RPs, check fine print)
  • Annual Maximum (How much total coverage do you have? Usually $500-$3,000)

Need help? We can verify your coverage during your consultation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a doctor's referral?

No. You do not need a referral to see a Registered Psychotherapist in Ontario. You can book directly.

How does reimbursement work?

After each session, you receive a receipt with our license number (CRPO #10979). Submit this photo/PDF to your insurance app (Sun Life, Manulife, etc.), and they typically deposit funds within 24-48 hours.

Can I claim therapy on my taxes?

Yes! If you pay out-of-pocket, psychotherapy fees are eligible medical expenses on your CRA tax return.
