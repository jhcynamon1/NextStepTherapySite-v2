Anxiety & Stress Therapy

Professional support for managing anxiety, stress, and overwhelming feelings that impact your daily life and wellbeing

Understanding Anxiety and Stress

Anxiety and stress are natural human responses, but when they become overwhelming or persistent, they can significantly impact your quality of life, relationships, and daily functioning.

You might be experiencing anxiety if you find yourself constantly worrying, feeling on edge, or avoiding certain situations. Stress might manifest as feeling overwhelmed, having difficulty concentrating, or experiencing physical symptoms like tension headaches or sleep disturbances.

The good news is that anxiety and stress are highly treatable conditions. With the right support and evidence-based therapeutic approaches, you can learn effective strategies to manage these challenges and reclaim your sense of calm and control.

Common Signs You Might Benefit from Support:

  • Persistent worry or racing thoughts
  • Physical symptoms (rapid heartbeat, sweating, tension)
  • Avoiding situations due to anxiety
  • Difficulty sleeping or concentrating
  • Feeling overwhelmed by daily responsibilities
  • Panic attacks or intense fear episodes
  • Social anxiety or fear of judgment
  • Perfectionism or fear of making mistakes

Types of Anxiety We Support

Generalized Anxiety

Persistent, excessive worry about various aspects of life that interferes with daily activities and relationships.

Social Anxiety

Intense fear of social situations, being judged by others, or embarrassing yourself in public settings.

Panic Disorder

Recurring panic attacks with intense physical symptoms and fear of having future attacks.

Stress-Related Anxiety

Anxiety stemming from life transitions, relationship challenges, health concerns, or major life changes.

Our Evidence-Based Treatment Approach

At Next Step Therapy, we use proven therapeutic methods tailored to your unique needs and circumstances. Our approach focuses on both immediate relief and long-term resilience building.

Therapeutic Modalities We Use:

  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT): Learn to accept difficult thoughts and feelings while taking action aligned with your values
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Identify and change unhelpful thought patterns that contribute to anxiety
  • Mindfulness-Based Approaches: Develop present-moment awareness and emotional regulation skills
  • Exposure and Response Prevention: Gradually face feared situations in a safe, supportive environment

What You Can Expect:

  • Personalized treatment plan based on your specific needs
  • Practical coping strategies you can use immediately
  • Understanding of your anxiety triggers and patterns
  • Tools for long-term emotional regulation and resilience
  • Support in developing healthy lifestyle habits
  • Flexible session scheduling (virtual and in-person options)

What to Expect in Therapy

Initial Sessions (1-3):

  • Comprehensive assessment of your anxiety and stress patterns
  • Discussion of your goals and preferences
  • Development of your personalized treatment plan
  • Introduction to initial coping strategies

Ongoing Therapy (4-12+ sessions):

  • Working through specific anxiety triggers and situations
  • Learning and practicing new coping skills
  • Gradual exposure to feared situations (when appropriate)
  • Processing and integration of new insights

Timeline and Progress:

First 2-4 weeks: Many clients notice initial relief as they begin to understand their anxiety patterns and learn basic coping strategies.

6-8 weeks: Most clients report significant improvement in their ability to manage anxiety and stress in daily situations.

3-6 months: Development of long-term resilience and confidence in managing anxiety independently.

Remember: Everyone's journey is unique. We'll work at your pace and adjust our approach based on your progress and feedback.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does anxiety therapy typically take?

Most clients see meaningful improvement within 6-12 sessions, though this varies based on individual circumstances. Some people benefit from short-term focused work (8-16 sessions), while others prefer longer-term support. We'll discuss your goals and preferences to determine what feels right for you.

Will I need to take medication for my anxiety?

As a psychotherapist, I don't prescribe medication, but many clients find therapy alone to be very effective for managing anxiety. If you're interested in exploring medication options, I can help you connect with appropriate medical professionals and work collaboratively with your healthcare team.

What's the difference between anxiety and stress?

Stress is typically a response to external pressures or demands, while anxiety often involves worry about future events or situations. Both can cause similar physical and emotional symptoms, and they often occur together. In therapy, we address both patterns and develop strategies that work for your specific experience.

Is virtual therapy as effective as in-person sessions?

Research shows that virtual therapy can be just as effective as in-person therapy for anxiety and stress-related concerns. Many clients appreciate the convenience and comfort of attending sessions from their own space. We can discuss which format feels most comfortable for you.
