## üéØ Key Insights for Content Strategy

### What This Confirms:

1. **"Specialize in" language matters**
   - People search for specialists, not generalists
   - "Who specializes in X" = high-intent phrase
   - Should use "I specialize in..." more prominently

2. **"Managing stress" vs "treating stress"**
   - Users frame it as "managing" (active, ongoing)
   - Not "curing" or "fixing" (implies broken)
   - Matches ACT philosophy (managing, not eliminating)

3. **"Demanding work roles" resonates**
   - Not just "stressful jobs"
   - "Demanding" = high-stakes, high-responsibility
   - "Roles" = identity/career, not just tasks

4. **Job-specific positioning works**
   - They wanted someone who understands work context
   - Not generic "anxiety therapy"
   - Workplace specialization = competitive advantage

## üí° Why This Matters

**Most SEO advice says:** "Target high-volume keywords"

**Reality:**
- "Therapist near me" = 10,000 searches/month, 0.5% CTR = 50 clicks
- "Managing stress demanding work roles" = 50 searches/month, 15% CTR = 7.5 clicks
- BUT: Second query converts at 50%, first converts at 5%
- Result: 3.75 bookings vs 2.5 bookings

**Long-tail, specific queries = lower volume but higher conversion.**

This client proves it:
- They searched a super specific 8-word phrase
- Found us (even though we don't use exact phrase much)
- Booked immediately (no shopping around)
- High intent = high conversion

**Strategic takeaway:**
Don't chase "therapist toronto" (massive competition, low intent).
Chase "managing stress in demanding work roles" (low competition, high intent).
