Whether you're dealing with generalized anxiety disorder, panic attacks, social anxiety, or specific phobias, it's important to know that anxiety is highly treatable. Research consistently shows that psychotherapy, particularly approaches like Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), can be incredibly effective in helping people develop a healthier relationship with anxiety and reclaim their lives.

My Therapeutic Approach: Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

Rather than trying to eliminate or control anxiety â€“ which often makes it worse â€“ ACT helps you develop psychological flexibility: the ability to stay present with difficult emotions while taking meaningful action in your life.

ACT is recognized by the American Psychological Association as an evidence-based treatment for anxiety disorders. It combines mindfulness strategies with behavior change techniques to help you live a rich, full life even when anxiety is present. This approach is particularly effective because it doesn't require you to "fix" yourself or eliminate anxiety completely â€“ instead, it teaches you how to respond differently to anxious thoughts and feelings.

Core ACT Components for Anxiety Recovery

1

Mindfulness & Present Moment Awareness

Learn to observe your anxious thoughts and feelings without being overwhelmed by them. Develop the ability to stay grounded in the present moment rather than getting caught up in future worries or past regrets.

2

Cognitive Defusion

Discover techniques to step back from anxious thoughts and see them for what they are â€“ just thoughts, not facts. This helps reduce the power anxiety has over your decisions and actions.

3

Values Clarification

Identify what truly matters to you in life â€“ your core values and what gives your life meaning. This becomes your compass for making decisions, rather than letting anxiety be in the driver's seat.

4

Committed Action

Take steps toward what matters to you, even when anxiety is present. Build confidence through values-based action rather than waiting for anxiety to disappear before living your life.

5

Acceptance & Psychological Flexibility

Develop the ability to experience anxiety without fighting it or being controlled by it. This paradoxically reduces anxiety's impact and increases your freedom to choose how you respond.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does anxiety therapy typically take?

Most clients see significant improvement within 8-16 sessions, though this varies depending on the severity and complexity of your anxiety. ACT is typically a shorter-term therapy focused on skill-building and practical change. We'll discuss progress regularly and adjust the treatment length as needed.

Will I need to face my fears directly?

While exposure can be part of treatment, ACT takes a gentler approach than traditional exposure therapy. We focus first on building psychological flexibility and coping skills. Any exposure work is gradual, collaborative, and always within your comfort zone for growth.

Can therapy help with physical anxiety symptoms?

Absolutely. ACT includes mindfulness and acceptance techniques that specifically address the physical experience of anxiety. Many clients find that as they change their relationship with anxiety, physical symptoms naturally decrease in intensity and frequency.

Do you prescribe medication for anxiety?

As a Registered Psychotherapist, I don't prescribe medication. However, I work collaboratively with your family doctor or psychiatrist if medication is part of your treatment plan. Many anxiety conditions respond very well to therapy alone.

Is virtual therapy effective for anxiety treatment?

Research shows that online therapy can be just as effective as in-person treatment for anxiety disorders. Virtual sessions offer the added benefit of allowing you to practice anxiety management skills in your own environment, which can enhance real-world application.
