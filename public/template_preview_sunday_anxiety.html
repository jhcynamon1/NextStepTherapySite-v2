Understanding the Sunday Scaries

Why Sunday Nights Hit Different

Sunday night anxiety isn't just "being dramatic" about Monday. It's your nervous system's anticipatory response to perceived threats. When work has become associated with stress, pressure, or past negative experiences, your brain starts preparing for battle before the week even begins.

This anticipatory anxiety often stems from:

  • Unresolved workplace stress that accumulates over time
  • Perfectionism and fear of not meeting expectations
  • Lack of boundaries between work and personal life
  • Past experiences of Monday morning criticism or conflict
  • Feeling trapped in a role that doesn't align with your values
  • The jarring transition from weekend freedom to weekday structure

The cruel irony? The anxiety about Monday often ruins the very weekend that's supposed to help you recharge. It becomes a self-perpetuating cycle where you enter each week already depleted.

How Therapy Can Help

Sunday night anxiety is highly responsive to therapy. Together, we work on both immediate coping strategies and addressing the underlying patterns that fuel your anticipatory stress.

Our Approach Focuses On:

  • Identifying specific triggers and thought patterns that intensify Sunday anxiety
  • Developing practical tools for managing anticipatory stress in the moment
  • Exploring your relationship with work and what it represents
  • Building sustainable boundaries between work and personal life
  • Processing past workplace experiences that may be influencing present anxiety
  • Creating rituals and practices that reclaim your weekends

Many clients report that simply having a space to process work stress without judgment helps break the isolation that Sunday night anxiety creates. You don't have to white-knuckle through another Sunday evening.

What to Expect

Starting therapy for Sunday night anxiety begins with understanding your unique experience. We'll explore when it started, what makes it worse, and what (if anything) provides relief. This isn't about quick fixes or positive thinking - it's about developing a deeper understanding of your anxiety and building sustainable strategies.
