Mental Health Challenges Look Different for Men

In a city like London, with its mix of academic, healthcare, and business pressures, the expectation is often to 'just push through.' But stress, anxiety, and depression often show up differently for men:

  • Anxiety as Irritability: Feeling angry or on-edge instead of obviously worried.
  • Depression as Numbness: Feeling disconnected or losing interest in things you used to enjoy.
  • Work Stress as Avoidance: Using alcohol, overworking, or other coping mechanisms to avoid dealing with the root issue.
  • Physical Symptoms: Headaches, muscle tension, or trouble sleeping are often the first signs something is wrong.

Recognizing these signs is a sign of strength. It's the first step toward getting practical tools to manage them effectively.
