Parenting Wasn't Supposed to Feel This Hard

You love your kids fiercely, but some days you barely recognize yourself. Between work demands, household management, and keeping tiny humans alive and happy, there's no time left for you.

Virtual therapy lets you prioritize your mental health without adding another impossible item to your already overwhelming schedule.

Parent-Specific Mental Health Challenges

Mom/Dad Guilt

Constant worry that you're not doing enough, being enough, or giving your children what they need to thrive.

Identity Loss

Feeling like you've completely lost yourself in the demands of parenting and can't remember who you used to be.

Partner Relationship Strain

Parenting stress affecting your relationship - feeling like roommates instead of partners, constant conflict over parenting approaches.

Overwhelm and Burnout

Running on empty with no time to recharge. Feeling exhausted but unable to rest because the demands never stop.

Postpartum Struggles

Difficulty adjusting to new baby life, postpartum anxiety or depression, or challenges with additional children after the first.

Work-Parent Balance

Juggling career demands with family responsibilities, feeling like you're failing at both roles simultaneously.

Therapy That Fits Your Real Life

Sessions during nap time. Late evening after kids are in bed. Early morning before the household wakes up. Weekend appointments when your partner can watch the kids.

No more arranging childcare or driving across town. Just you, your computer, and 50 minutes of uninterrupted support.

How Virtual Parent Therapy Works

Flexible Scheduling

Book sessions around school schedules, nap times, or whenever you can carve out 50 minutes. Evening and weekend appointments available.

Interruption-Friendly

Kids inevitably interrupt - that's real life. Sessions can be paused if needed, and we work around the beautiful chaos of family life.

Privacy Protection

Secure, encrypted video sessions that protect your confidentiality even when you're discussing sensitive family matters from home.

Parent-Focused Strategies

Practical coping skills you can use in real-time during tantrums, bedtime battles, or overwhelming moments with your children.

What We Work On Together

  • Stress management techniques you can use while kids are melting down
  • Setting boundaries with extended family, schools, and other parents
  • Rediscovering your identity beyond being "mom" or "dad"
  • Partner communication about parenting approaches and relationship needs
  • Managing parenting anxiety about your children's safety, development, or future
  • Creating sustainable routines that work for your family's unique needs

Virtual Sessions: The Parent-Friendly Choice

Traditional therapy often doesn't work for busy parents. Here's why virtual sessions make sense:

No Childcare Required

Sessions during school hours, nap times, or when kids are with the other parent. No need to arrange and pay for babysitting.

Zero Commute Time

Save 30-60 minutes of driving time per session. That's time you can spend on self-care or family activities instead.

Familiar Environment

Discuss parenting challenges from your own space where the stress actually happens. More authentic and immediate support.

Weather-Proof Sessions

Snow days, sick kids, or unexpected school closures don't derail your therapy schedule.
