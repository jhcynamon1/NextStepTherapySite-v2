Comprehensive Depression Treatment in London, Ontario

Depression affects approximately 11% of Canadians during their lifetime, but effective treatment is available. At Next Step Therapy, we provide professional depression therapy services to residents of London, Ontario and surrounding communities including St. Thomas, Strathroy, and Woodstock.

Our evidence-based approach combines proven therapeutic methods with compassionate, non-judgmental support. Whether you're experiencing major depression, persistent low mood, seasonal depression, or depression related to life changes, we're here to help you find your way back to wellness and meaning.

Evidence-Based Depression Treatment Approaches

>

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is one of the most effective treatments for depression. We help you identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors that maintain depressive symptoms, developing healthier coping strategies and thinking patterns.

<

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

ACT helps you develop psychological flexibility and learn to accept difficult emotions while staying committed to your values, even during challenging times. This approach is particularly effective for persistent depression.

Behavioral Activation

A structured approach that focuses on increasing meaningful, value-based activities to combat depression. We help you gradually re-engage with life through planned, rewarding activities.

>

Mindfulness-Based Approaches

Learn mindfulness techniques that help you develop a different relationship with depressive thoughts and feelings, reducing rumination and increasing present-moment awareness.

=

Interpersonal Therapy Techniques

Address relationship patterns and interpersonal difficulties that may contribute to depression, improving communication skills and social connections.

=

Relapse Prevention

Develop personalized strategies to maintain wellness and prevent future depressive episodes, including early warning sign recognition and coping plan development.

Recognizing Depression Symptoms

Depression affects everyone differently. If you're experiencing several of these symptoms for two weeks or more, professional support can help.

= Persistent Sadness

Ongoing feelings of sadness, emptiness, or hopelessness that don't improve

= Sleep Changes

Insomnia, early morning waking, or sleeping too much

< Loss of Interest

Reduced interest or pleasure in activities you once enjoyed

Fatigue

Persistent tiredness and loss of energy, even with adequate rest

< Appetite Changes

Significant changes in appetite or weight

> Concentration Difficulties

Trouble focusing, remembering, or making decisions

= Negative Thoughts

Persistent feelings of worthlessness, guilt, or self-criticism

= Physical Symptoms

Unexplained aches, pains, or physical slowing

Types of Depression We Treat in London, Ontario

Major Depressive Disorder

Severe depressive symptoms that significantly impact daily functioning, relationships, and quality of life for extended periods.

Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)

Chronic, long-term depression lasting two years or more, often with periods of major depression mixed with milder symptoms.

Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)

Depression that occurs during specific seasons, typically winter months with reduced sunlight exposure.

Postpartum Depression

Depression occurring after childbirth, affecting bonding, daily functioning, and overall well-being of new parents.

Depression with Anxiety

Co-occurring depression and anxiety symptoms that require integrated treatment approaches addressing both conditions.

Adjustment Disorder with Depression

Depression triggered by specific life events or changes, such as job loss, relationship ending, or major transitions.

Treatment-Resistant Depression

Depression that hasn't responded adequately to previous treatment attempts, requiring specialized therapeutic approaches.

Depression in Older Adults

Late-life depression often complicated by medical conditions, grief, isolation, and age-related life changes.
