Types of Depression We Treat in London, Ontario

Major Depressive Disorder

Severe depressive symptoms that significantly impact daily functioning, relationships, and quality of life for extended periods.

Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)

Chronic, long-term depression lasting two years or more, often with periods of major depression mixed with milder symptoms.

Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)

Depression that occurs during specific seasons, typically winter months with reduced sunlight exposure.

Postpartum Depression

Depression occurring after childbirth, affecting bonding, daily functioning, and overall well-being of new parents.

Depression with Anxiety

Co-occurring depression and anxiety symptoms that require integrated treatment approaches addressing both conditions.

Adjustment Disorder with Depression

Depression triggered by specific life events or changes, such as job loss, relationship ending, or major transitions.

Treatment-Resistant Depression

Depression that hasn't responded adequately to previous treatment attempts, requiring specialized therapeutic approaches.

Depression in Older Adults

Late-life depression often complicated by medical conditions, grief, isolation, and age-related life changes.
