You keep waiting to feel like yourself again. There was a time when you had more energy, more interest, more... something. You're not sure when it changed, but this version of you doesn't feel right. And you're wondering if this is just what life is now.

If any of this lands, you're not weak. Depression is real. And you don't have to figure it out alone.

How Depression Therapy Actually Helps

Depression isn't a character flaw or a weakness. It's a real condition that affects how you think, feel, and function. Therapy doesn't magically fix it overnight. But it gives you tools and perspective that can genuinely change things.

Here's what I've seen working with clients from Durham Region: depression often makes sense in context. There are real pressures. Real exhaustion. Real reasons why you feel the way you do. The problem is that depression distorts everything. It tells you nothing will work, nothing matters, nothing will change. That voice is loud and convincing. But it's not accurate.

I use Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), which has strong research support. The focus isn't on forcing yourself to feel better. It's on reconnecting with what matters to you and taking small steps in that direction, even when motivation is low.

What we typically work on:

  • Understanding what depression is actually doing to your thinking and energy
  • Identifying what you actually value, not just what you "should" want
  • Taking meaningful action even when motivation is absent
  • Developing a different relationship with difficult thoughts and feelings
  • Rebuilding a sense of purpose and connection

The goal isn't to never feel sad or tired. The goal is to stop depression from running your life.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does depression therapy cost in Whitby?

Sessions are $175 each. I provide insurance receipts for all major Ontario insurance providers. Many Whitby residents have coverage through employer benefits that cover psychotherapy.

Do you offer in-person therapy in Whitby?

I offer virtual therapy exclusively, which works well for Durham Region residents. When motivation is already low, not having to commute to an appointment removes a real barrier.

How quickly can I get an appointment?

Most clients get their first session within the same week. I know Durham Region waitlists can be months long. When you're ready to start, you shouldn't have to wait.

What type of therapy do you use for depression?

I use Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), an evidence-based approach. Instead of trying to eliminate difficult feelings, we work on building a life that feels meaningful even when things are hard.

Can therapy help when I feel like nothing will work?

That feeling is actually a symptom of depression. Your brain is telling you nothing will help as a way to protect you from disappointment. But that same brain is also filtering out evidence that things can change.

What if I've never done therapy before?

Most people I work with haven't. The first session is about getting to know each other. You don't need to have anything figured out. That's my job to help guide.
