Evidence-Based Depression Therapy in Ontario

Depression isn't just feeling sadâ€”it's a complex experience that affects your thoughts, emotions, physical energy, and daily functioning. Whether you're dealing with persistent low mood, loss of interest in activities, difficulty concentrating, sleep disruption, or feelings of worthlessness, professional therapy can help you understand and work through these challenges.

The approach I use is Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), which has extensive research support for depression. Rather than trying to eliminate difficult emotions entirely, ACT helps you develop a different relationship with depression so it doesn't control your life choices and daily actions.

Depression Often Shows Up As:

  • Persistent Low Mood: Feeling sad, empty, or hopeless most days
  • Loss of Interest: Activities that used to be enjoyable feel pointless or exhausting
  • Energy Depletion: Feeling tired even after sleeping, or struggling to get out of bed
  • Negative Self-Talk: Harsh inner criticism and feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Concentration Difficulties: Trouble focusing at work, reading, or making decisions
  • Sleep Disruption: Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or sleeping too much
  • Social Withdrawal: Avoiding friends, family, or activities you used to enjoy
  • Physical Symptoms: Headaches, digestive issues, or unexplained aches and pains

If several of these experiences describe your recent months, you're not broken or weakâ€”you're experiencing something that affects millions of people. Depression is treatable, and with the right support, you can feel more like yourself again.

What to Expect in Your First Session

Starting therapy when you're depressed can feel daunting. Here's what the process looks like, designed to be as comfortable and supportive as possible:

Free 15-Minute Consultation

We'll start with a brief phone call where you can share what you're experiencing and ask any questions. This helps us both determine if we're a good fit before scheduling a full session. There's no pressure and no commitment beyond this initial conversation.

Your First Full Session (50 minutes)

During your first session, we'll explore your specific experience with depression. What does it feel like in your body? When did you first notice these changes? How has it been affecting your daily life, work, and relationships? We'll also discuss your goals for therapy and what you hope will be different.

Building Your Support Plan: Sessions 2-6

Together, we'll develop practical strategies you can use immediately:

  • Behavioral Activation: Gradually re-engaging with activities that bring meaning and pleasure
  • Mindfulness Skills: Learning to observe difficult thoughts and emotions without being overwhelmed by them
  • Values Clarification: Identifying what truly matters to you so you can make choices aligned with your values
  • Cognitive Flexibility: Developing a different relationship with depressive thoughts
  • Self-Compassion: Learning to treat yourself with kindness during difficult times

Ongoing Support and Growth

As you practice these approaches, we'll refine them based on what works best for your specific situation and lifestyle. Some people prefer weekly sessions initially, while others find biweekly sessions more manageable. We'll find a rhythm that supports your progress without adding stress to your life.

Between sessions, you're welcome to email with questions or observations. While I can't provide therapy via email, I can offer brief clarification about techniques we've discussed or support during particularly difficult moments.
