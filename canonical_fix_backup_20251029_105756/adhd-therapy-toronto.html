Understanding Adult ADHD

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) affects many adults in Toronto and across Ontario. While often identified in childhood, many adults discover they have ADHD later in life, which can bring both relief and challenges.

Adult ADHD symptoms may include:

  • Difficulty maintaining focus on tasks or conversations
  • Problems with organization and time management
  • Procrastination and difficulty completing projects
  • Impulsivity in decision-making or relationships
  • Restlessness or feeling constantly "on the go"
  • Difficulty managing emotions and stress
  • Challenges in work performance or career advancement
  • Relationship difficulties due to ADHD symptoms

The Impact of Undiagnosed ADHD

Many adults with ADHD have spent years feeling different or struggling without understanding why. This can lead to:

  • Low self-esteem and feelings of inadequacy
  • Anxiety and depression secondary to ADHD challenges
  • Difficulties in academic or professional settings
  • Strained relationships and social difficulties
  • Chronic stress from trying to manage symptoms alone

Our ADHD therapy in Toronto focuses on both addressing symptoms and healing from the impact of years of struggling without proper support.

Evidence-Based ADHD Therapy Approaches

Our ADHD therapy services in Toronto incorporate multiple therapeutic approaches proven effective for adults with ADHD:

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy for ADHD

CBT helps identify and change negative thought patterns that often develop alongside ADHD. We work on building practical skills for managing symptoms while addressing anxiety, depression, or low self-esteem that may accompany ADHD.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

ACT helps you accept your ADHD while committing to actions that align with your values. This approach is particularly effective for managing the emotional aspects of ADHD and building psychological flexibility.

Mindfulness-Based Interventions

Mindfulness practices can significantly improve attention regulation, emotional control, and self-awareness. These skills are particularly valuable for adults with ADHD who struggle with focus and emotional regulation.

Skills Training and Coaching

Practical skill-building sessions focus on executive functioning, including:

  • Time management and scheduling systems
  • Organization strategies for home and work
  • Task prioritization and completion techniques
  • Goal setting and achievement strategies
  • Emotional regulation techniques

Frequently Asked Questions About ADHD Therapy

Do I need an ADHD diagnosis to benefit from ADHD therapy?
While an official diagnosis can be helpful, you don't need one to benefit from ADHD-informed therapy. If you're experiencing ADHD-like symptoms, we can provide support and help you explore whether seeking an assessment might be beneficial.
Is ADHD therapy covered by insurance in Ontario?
Yes, therapy sessions with a Registered Psychotherapist are covered by most extended health insurance plans in Ontario. This includes ADHD-focused therapy and support services.
How is ADHD therapy different from regular therapy?
ADHD therapy is specifically adapted to how ADHD brains work. Sessions are often more interactive, practical, and skill-focused. The therapist understands ADHD challenges and builds on ADHD strengths rather than seeing ADHD as something to overcome.
Can therapy help with ADHD medication decisions?
While I cannot prescribe medication, therapy can complement medical treatment by helping you understand your ADHD better, develop coping strategies, and make informed decisions about treatment options in consultation with your healthcare provider.
How long does ADHD therapy typically take?
The length of therapy varies depending on your goals and needs. Some people benefit from short-term focused work on specific skills, while others prefer ongoing support. We'll work together to determine what approach works best for you.
Do you offer virtual ADHD therapy sessions?
Yes, we offer secure virtual therapy sessions which can be particularly convenient for adults with ADHD who may struggle with commuting or prefer the comfort of their own space.
