Understanding Grief as a Natural Process

Grief counseling recognizes that grief is not a problem to be fixed but a natural human response to loss. Each person's grief journey is unique, influenced by the relationship lost, circumstances of the loss, personal history, and individual coping styles.

Common grief experiences include:

  • Intense sadness and overwhelming emotions
  • Anger, guilt, and regret about the loss
  • Difficulty concentrating and making decisions
  • Physical symptoms and changes in sleep or appetite
  • Social withdrawal and isolation from others
  • Questioning beliefs and searching for meaning

Types of Loss Addressed in Grief Therapy

Bereavement therapy supports individuals through various types of loss, recognizing that grief extends beyond death to include many forms of significant loss and transition.

Types of loss we address:

  • Death of a loved one (family member, friend, pet)
  • Relationship endings and divorce
  • Job loss and career transitions
  • Health changes and loss of physical abilities
  • Loss of dreams, goals, or life expectations
  • Geographic moves and loss of community

Therapeutic Approach to Grief Support

Our grief counseling utilizes Acceptance and Commitment Therapy principles adapted for loss experiences. This approach helps you develop a healthier relationship with grief while building capacity for meaningful living alongside your loss.

Key therapeutic elements include:

  • Accepting grief as a natural part of loving and living
  • Developing skills for carrying grief with greater ease
  • Creating meaningful connections with the deceased or lost
  • Identifying and pursuing values-based activities
  • Building support systems and community connections
  • Processing complicated or traumatic aspects of loss

Moving Through Grief, Not Moving On

Modern grief therapy recognizes that the goal is not to "get over" grief but to learn how to carry it in a way that allows for continued living and meaning-making. This approach honors your ongoing connection to what or whom you've lost.

Virtual Grief Counseling Support

Access grief counseling through secure virtual sessions that provide comfort and convenience during a difficult time. Virtual support can be particularly beneficial when mobility is limited or when you need the comfort of your own space.

Complicated Grief and Additional Support

Some individuals experience complicated grief that significantly interferes with daily functioning for extended periods. Professional bereavement therapy can help identify when additional support or specialized interventions may be beneficial.

Creating Meaning and Continuing Bonds

Grief support often involves finding ways to maintain meaningful connections with what you've lost while building new sources of meaning and purpose. This might include rituals, memorials, or ways of honoring your loss.

Support for Family and Friends

Grief counseling can also provide guidance for family members and friends who want to better support someone who is grieving, helping them understand the grief process and how to offer meaningful support.
